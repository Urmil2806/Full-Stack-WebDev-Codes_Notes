Twitter vs Threads: Comparing data collection practices of microblogging platforms

By Aakash Sharma: Threads, Meta's answer to Elon Musk's Twitter, is set to be launched on July 6 amid the raging debate on how the upcoming application will collect user data. Netizens are pointing at privacy concerns of Threads, saying the app is collecting more user-related data than Twitter. A preview of Instagram's text-based conversation app is now available on the App Store in India that allows us to take a deeper look into data collection by the two microblogging platforms. Threads may not have a simultaneous launch in EU countries due to stricter privacy rules. Ireland's Data Protection Commission has reportedly said that Meta's new service may not be rolled out in the EU at this time.

Threads vs Twitter: Data Collection

India Today's OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence) team analyses the data collection practices of both the microblogging platforms, Twitter and Threads, and unearthed a significant discrepancy. Of the 25 categories of varying user- generated information, Threads was found to collect additional data that Twitter does not. Notably, Threads collects some sensitive information such as physical address, health and fitness data, and Other User Contact Info and other sensitive information within the app.
